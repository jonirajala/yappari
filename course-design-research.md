# Japanese Beginner Course Design Research
## For learners who already know hiragana and katakana

---

## 1. JLPT N5 Level Scope

### What N5 Covers
- ~800 vocabulary words
- ~100 kanji
- ~80 grammar points
- ~150-300 hours of study (3-6 months of consistent daily practice)
- Covers basic daily conversation, simple questions, and fundamental expressions

### Vocabulary Breakdown by Type
- Verbs: ~250-300 words
- i-adjectives and na-adjectives: ~150-200 words
- Nouns: covers food, places, time, people, objects
- Adverbs, particles, expressions: remainder

---

## 2. Recommended Vocabulary Category Progression

Based on how Duolingo, LingoDeer, and Genki structure their courses, the recommended order for introducing vocabulary categories is:

### Phase 1: Survival Basics (Lessons 1-10)
1. **Greetings & Set Phrases** - おはようございます、こんにちは、ありがとう、すみません、はい、いいえ
2. **Self-introduction** - 私、名前、～です、～じゃないです
3. **Numbers 1-10** - 一、二、三、四、五、六、七、八、九、十
4. **Pronouns & Demonstratives** - これ、それ、あれ、この、その、あの

### Phase 2: People & Places (Lessons 11-25)
5. **People & Family** - 人、先生、学生、友達、お母さん、お父さん、兄、姉、弟、妹
6. **Common Objects** - 本、ペン、かばん、時計、電話、テーブル、いす
7. **Places** - 学校、家、駅、店、病院、レストラン、トイレ
8. **Food & Drink** - ご飯、水、お茶、肉、魚、野菜、パン、卵

### Phase 3: Daily Life (Lessons 26-45)
9. **Time Expressions** - 今日、明日、昨日、朝、昼、夜、～時、～分
10. **Days of the Week** - 月曜日～日曜日
11. **Basic Verbs** - 食べる、飲む、行く、来る、見る、聞く、話す、読む、書く、する
12. **Common Adjectives** - 大きい、小さい、新しい、古い、高い、安い、おいしい、きれい

### Phase 4: Getting Around (Lessons 46-65)
13. **Transportation** - 電車、バス、車、自転車、駅
14. **Directions** - 右、左、前、後ろ、上、下、隣
15. **Shopping** - いくら、円、高い、安い、～をください
16. **Weather & Seasons** - 天気、暑い、寒い、春、夏、秋、冬

### Phase 5: Expanding Communication (Lessons 66-90)
17. **Hobbies & Activities** - 映画、音楽、スポーツ、旅行、写真
18. **Body & Health** - 頭、目、手、足、元気、病気
19. **Colors** - 赤、青、白、黒、緑
20. **Counters** - ～つ、～人、～匹、～本、～枚

## 3. Grammar Progression (Recommended Teaching Order)

Based on Genki, Bunpro, Tae Kim's Guide, and JLPT N5 requirements:

### Tier 1: Absolute Foundations (Introduce First)
1. **だ / です** - copula "to be" (X は Y です)
2. **は** - topic marker particle
3. **か** - question particle
4. **の** - possession particle (私の本)
5. **も** - "also, too"
6. **これ / それ / あれ** - demonstrative pronouns
7. **この / その / あの** - demonstrative adjectives
8. **じゃない / ではない** - negative of だ/です

### Tier 2: Describing Things
9. **い-adjectives** - basic usage (大きい、小さい)
10. **な-adjectives** - basic usage (きれい、静か)
11. **い-adjective negation** - くない form
12. **い-adjective past** - かった form
13. **な-adjective + noun** - modification pattern
14. **い-adjective + noun** - modification pattern
15. **好き / 嫌い** - likes and dislikes (が好き)

### Tier 3: Existence & Location
16. **が** - subject marker particle
17. **に** - location/time particle
18. **がある / がいる** - existence (non-living / living)
19. **ここ / そこ / あそこ** - location pronouns
20. **で** - location of action particle

### Tier 4: Basic Verb Usage
21. **Verb groups** - る-verbs vs う-verbs recognition
22. **ます form** - polite present/future
23. **を** - object marker particle
24. **に / へ** - direction particles (行く、来る)
25. **で** - means/instrument particle
26. **Verb negative** - ません / ない
27. **Verb past** - ました / た
28. **Verb negative past** - ませんでした / なかった

### Tier 5: Connecting Ideas
29. **と** - "and" (nouns), "with"
30. **や** - "and" (non-exhaustive list)
31. **から** - "because" / "from"
32. **けど / けれども** - "but, however"
33. **でも** - "but, however" (sentence opener)
34. **ので** - "because, since"
35. **が** - "but" (conjunction)

### Tier 6: て-form & Its Uses (Major Milestone)
36. **て-form construction** - conjugation rules
37. **てください** - polite requests
38. **てもいい** - permission ("may I")
39. **てはいけない** - prohibition ("must not")
40. **ている** - ongoing action / states
41. **てから** - "after doing"

### Tier 7: Wants, Plans & Suggestions
42. **たい** - "want to do"
43. **ほしい** - "want something"
44. **つもりだ** - "plan to / intend to"
45. **ましょう / ましょうか** - "let's / shall we"
46. **ませんか** - invitation ("won't you")
47. **ほうがいい** - "had better / should"

### Tier 8: More Patterns
48. **たことがある** - "have done before" (experience)
49. **たり～たりする** - "doing things like X and Y"
50. **前に** - "before"
51. **まで** - "until"
52. **だけ** - "only, just"
53. **まだ / もう** - "still / already"
54. **なくてはいけない** - "must do"

### Tier 9: Comparisons & Advanced Description
55. **より～の方が** - comparison ("more than")
56. **一番** - superlative ("the most")
57. **すぎる** - "too much"
58. **になる / くなる** - "to become"
59. **にする** - "to decide on"
60. **のが上手 / 下手** - "good at / bad at"

## 4. How Duolingo Structures Japanese

### Overall Architecture
- **221 units** across **5 sections** (Rookie, Explorer, Traveler, Trailblazer, Champion)
- 6,889 total words taught
- 569 distinct lessons
- Each lesson has 1-18 exercises (usually ~15), taking 5-10 minutes

### Section Breakdown

**Section 1: Rookie (Units 1-10) - Pre-N5**
- Order food, Describe people, Introduce yourself
- Order food and drink, Talk about countries
- Ask for directions, Describe belongings
- Talk about neighbors, Tell time, Get help traveling

**Section 2: Explorer (Units 11-45) - N5 Level**
- Family, hobbies, eating habits, shopping
- Present tense verbs, station navigation, daily routine
- Home, public transit, na-adjectives
- Weather, chores, wants, negative verbs
- Jobs, media, seasonal events, counting

**Section 3: Traveler (Units 46-136) - N4 Preparation**
- Past tense verbs, shopping, planning
- Describing experiences, conversational skills
- Integrated stories begin appearing (units 76-85)

**Section 4: Trailblazer (Units 137-216) - N4/N3**
- Conditional forms, passive/causative
- Story-based learning units

**Section 5: Champion (Units 217-221) - B1/N3**
- Opinions, nature, news, feelings, seasons

### Key Design Principles
- Functional/situational topics (ordering food, getting directions)
- Grammar introduced implicitly through context
- Heavy repetition with spaced intervals
- Short, daily-practice-oriented sessions

## 5. How LingoDeer Structures Japanese

### Course Philosophy
- Covers A1-B1 levels (roughly Genki I and II scope)
- 90% of grammar from beginner textbooks
- Explicit grammar explanations (unlike Duolingo)
- Small chunks of learning, larger chunks of review

### Lesson Flow
1. New vocabulary introduced with pictures + captions (kana, kanji, romaji)
2. Basic matching exercises to establish recognition
3. 2-3 new items introduced at a time
4. Immediate practice with fill-in-the-blank and matching
5. Progressive difficulty within each lesson
6. Grammar explanations appear in-line with context

### Review System
- "Review" tab: choose Vocabulary or Grammar review
- Can review all or filter by lesson category
- "5-Min Quiz" for quick refreshers
- Spaced repetition for long-term retention

### Exercise Types Used by LingoDeer
- Matching (word to meaning)
- Fill-in-the-blank
- Listening comprehension
- Reading comprehension
- Grammar-focused exercises
- Quiz/review exercises

### Unique Strengths
- Explicit grammar explanations with context and usage notes
- Polite vs. casual register noted
- Particle explanations
- Verb conjugation breakdowns
- Native speaker audio (not TTS)

## 6. Exercise Types for Japanese Learning

### Recommended Exercise Types (ranked by effectiveness for Japanese)

#### High Priority (Core Exercise Types)
1. **Multiple Choice (Recognition)**
   - Show Japanese, pick English meaning (or vice versa)
   - Show audio, pick the correct text
   - Best for: vocabulary introduction, kanji reading

2. **Word/Sentence Ordering (Production)**
   - Given scrambled words, arrange into correct Japanese sentence
   - Critical for Japanese because word order + particles matter
   - Best for: grammar patterns, particle usage, sentence structure
   - This is a classic JLPT exercise format

3. **Matching (Pairing)**
   - Match Japanese words to English meanings
   - Match kana to kanji
   - Best for: rapid vocabulary review, kana-kanji association

4. **Fill-in-the-Blank (Contextual)**
   - Complete a sentence with the missing word/particle
   - Excellent for particles (は, が, を, に, で)
   - Best for: grammar accuracy, particle usage

5. **Listening Comprehension**
   - Listen to audio, select correct text or meaning
   - Listen and type what you hear
   - Best for: pronunciation, listening skill, kana reinforcement

#### Medium Priority (Supporting Exercise Types)
6. **Translation (Full Production)**
   - English sentence -> construct Japanese equivalent
   - Most challenging exercise type
   - Best for: advanced grammar practice, testing production ability

7. **Reading Comprehension**
   - Read a short passage, answer questions about it
   - Best for: contextual vocabulary, grammar in context

8. **Kanji Reading Selection**
   - Given kanji, select the correct reading
   - Given a reading, select the correct kanji
   - Best for: kanji acquisition

#### Lower Priority (Supplementary)
9. **Typing/Free Input**
   - Type the answer in Japanese
   - Hard on mobile but powerful for retention
   - Best for: active recall, production practice

10. **Image Association**
    - See an image, select or type the Japanese word
    - Best for: concrete nouns, early vocabulary

### Japanese-Specific Exercise Considerations
- **Particle drills** are essential and unique to Japanese
- **Conjugation practice** (verb forms: ます, て, た, ない) needs dedicated exercises
- **Counter word exercises** (～つ、～人、～本) are uniquely Japanese
- **Politeness level** exercises (です/ます vs plain form) important early
- **Word ordering** is more important in Japanese than in European languages because particles mark grammatical roles

## 7. Lesson Structure Template

Based on how successful apps structure individual lessons:

### Single Lesson (~5-10 minutes)
1. **Introduction** (1-2 min)
   - Grammar point explained briefly with example
   - 2-3 new vocabulary words with images/audio

2. **Recognition Phase** (2-3 min)
   - Multiple choice: identify new words
   - Matching: pair words with meanings
   - Listening: hear word, select correct option

3. **Production Phase** (2-3 min)
   - Fill-in-the-blank with new grammar
   - Word ordering: build sentences
   - Translation: English to Japanese

4. **Consolidation** (1-2 min)
   - Mixed review of all items from this lesson
   - Possibly include 1-2 items from previous lessons

### Unit Structure (5-8 lessons per unit)
- Lesson 1-2: New vocabulary (themed)
- Lesson 3-4: New grammar point + vocabulary practice
- Lesson 5-6: Combined practice with listening
- Lesson 7-8: Review/quiz combining all unit material

## 9. Key Design Principles Summary

### From Research
1. **Topic-based vocabulary grouping** works better than random lists
2. **Grammar should be introduced when needed** for communication tasks, not in abstract
3. **Small learning chunks** (2-3 new items) with large review chunks
4. **Spaced repetition** is critical for retention
5. **80% known / 20% new** ratio per lesson keeps engagement high
6. **Multiple exercise types** per grammar point prevent boredom and test different skills
7. **Explicit grammar explanations** work better for Japanese than pure immersion (LingoDeer's advantage over Duolingo)
8. **Functional contexts** (ordering food, asking directions) provide motivation
9. **Native audio** is important for accurate pronunciation
10. **Progressive difficulty**: recognition -> production -> free production

### Japanese-Specific Design Notes
- Teach polite form (ます/です) FIRST, introduce plain form later (this is what Genki and most courses do)
- Particles are the hardest part for beginners - drill heavily
- て-form is the most important conjugation milestone - build up to it carefully
- Kanji should be introduced gradually alongside vocabulary, not separately
- Reading practice should start early since the learner already knows kana
- Since user already knows kana, the course can jump straight to vocabulary + basic grammar

## 9b. Kanji Introduction Strategy

### When to Introduce Kanji
**Start right after kana mastery** — this is the consensus across Genki, Tofugu, LingoDeer, and Duolingo.
- Genki introduces kanji in Lesson 3 (after hiragana/katakana in Lessons 1-2)
- LingoDeer shows kanji from the start with toggleable furigana
- Duolingo introduces kanji after Section 1 (~Unit 10+)
- Since our learners already know kana, we should begin kanji in Unit 3-4

### Progression Order (by theme, following Genki + JLPT N5)
1. **Numbers** (Unit 4): 一 二 三 四 五 六 七 八 九 十 百 千 万 円
2. **Time/days** (Unit 4): 日 月 火 水 木 金 土 時 年 今 分 半
3. **People** (Unit 3): 人 男 女 子 友 先 生 学
4. **Places/nature** (Unit 5): 山 川 国 天 気 店
5. **Size/direction** (Unit 5): 大 小 中 上 下 右 左 前 後 外
6. **Actions** (Unit 6-7): 食 飲 見 行 来 出 入 読 書 聞 話 買
7. **Adjectives** (Unit 6-7): 高 安 新 古 長 白

### Furigana Strategy
- **Units 1-2**: Pure kana (no kanji). Builds vocab foundation.
- **Units 3-4**: Introduce kanji WITH furigana always shown. User sees 学生 with がくせい above it.
- **Units 5+**: Furigana shown by default, toggleable off in settings.
- **Review exercises**: Can optionally hide furigana to test kanji reading.
- **Gold standard**: LingoDeer-style toggle (kana-only / kanji+furigana / kanji-only)

### How Apps Handle It
- **LingoDeer**: Kanji from the start, triple display (kanji + kana + romaji), user controls what's shown
- **Duolingo**: Kanji introduced after ~10 units, furigana toggle available
- **Genki**: Kanji from Lesson 3, ~15 per lesson, furigana in early chapters

### Implementation Notes
- Words should have both `kana` and `kanji` forms in data
- Display mode controlled by user setting + unit progression
- Kanji reading exercises (read kanji → pick kana) are a new exercise type
- ~100 kanji total for N5 level, ~15 per unit starting from Unit 3

### Sources
- Genki I introduces kanji Lesson 3: 一二三四五六七八九十百千万円時
- JLPT N5: ~100 kanji (no official list; estimates 78-112)
- Tofugu: "Start kanji immediately after kana"
- LingoDeer blog: 50 most used kanji list
- Renshuu.org: adaptive furigana hiding for "known" kanji

## 10. Proposed Course Outline (10 Units, ~80 Lessons)

### Unit 1: First Words (8 lessons)
- Greetings and set phrases
- Grammar: です、は、か
- Exercise focus: matching, multiple choice

### Unit 2: This and That (8 lessons)
- Demonstratives, common objects
- Grammar: これ/それ/あれ、この/その/あの、の (possession)
- Exercise focus: matching, image association

### Unit 3: People & Description (8 lessons)
- Family, people, first adjectives
- Grammar: い-adjectives, な-adjectives, も、じゃない
- Exercise focus: fill-in-blank, word ordering

### Unit 4: Numbers & Time (8 lessons)
- Numbers, time, days of the week
- Grammar: に (time), から...まで
- Exercise focus: listening, number practice

### Unit 5: Places & Existence (8 lessons)
- Locations, buildings, position words
- Grammar: がある/がいる、に (location), で (location of action)
- Exercise focus: fill-in-blank, sentence building

### Unit 6: Daily Actions (8 lessons)
- Common verbs, daily routine
- Grammar: ます form, を、に/へ (direction), で (means)
- Exercise focus: conjugation drills, word ordering

### Unit 7: Food & Ordering (8 lessons)
- Food vocabulary, restaurant phrases
- Grammar: を + verb、好き/嫌い、たい (want to)
- Exercise focus: situational dialogues, translation

### Unit 8: Connecting Ideas (8 lessons)
- More verbs, past tense
- Grammar: past tense, から、けど、ので
- Exercise focus: sentence building, translation

### Unit 9: Making Requests (8 lessons)
- Requests, permissions, obligations
- Grammar: て-form, てください、てもいい、てはいけない
- Exercise focus: conjugation, situational exercises

### Unit 10: Review & Beyond (8 lessons)
- Comprehensive review
- Grammar: ている、たことがある、comparison
- Exercise focus: mixed exercises, reading passages
